What would you do if you got struck by lightning? Once? Twice?
How about six times?
Meet Roy Sullivan, a park ranger with a crazy connection to lightning.
His first strike? 1942. He’s driving along, and—bam—a bolt hits him. He just walks away.
Second time? 1969. Walking in the park, lightning hits again. Knocked out cold, but somehow, he’s fine.
Now, you’d think he’d be done, right?
But no—he’s struck again in 1970.
And again in 1972. At this point, it’s like the sky’s got it out for him.
But it’s 1973 that gets wild.
Roy sees a storm coming, panics, and starts running for cover but the lighting hit him exactly where he ran. 
I know, you’re probably thinking, “How does this guy survive all that?”
Well, here’s the crazy part: After surviving six lightning strikes, it wasn’t lightning that ended Roy.
In 1983, he passed away, but not from a bolt of lightning…
He died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound.
Survived six lightning strikes, but in the end, fate caught up with him another way.
